About Me

Prasoon Choudhary is a seasoned IT project manager with over two decades of experience in designing, developing, and managing large-scale integrated eligibility systems for state government clients. He currently leads the Missouri MEDES SNAP project, where he has played a pivotal role in guiding the program from design through UAT, defect resolution, and operational readiness. His previous work spans multiple state implementations, including Arkansas, Utah, and Wisconsin, where he has earned a reputation for bridging policy, business, and technology needs with a practical and collaborative approach.

Prior to transitioning into project management, Prasoon worked as a developer, application architect, and solution architect, specializing in enterprise system design using Java technologies. He brings strong technical depth to his leadership role, with an interest in emerging tools like Python and an eye toward scalable, maintainable architectures. His ability to understand both the codebase and the strategic goals of a project allows him to effectively align technical implementation with business outcomes.

Outside of work, Prasoon is an avid runner and hiker, having completed multiple 5Ks, 10Ks, and a full marathon. He has a deep interest in physics and often spends time reading physics books and teaching the subject to his two daughters. His passion for science and lifelong learning carries over into both his personal and professional life, where he is known for his thoughtful mentorship, analytical mindset, and steady leadership in challenging projects.
